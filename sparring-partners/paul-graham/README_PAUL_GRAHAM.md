# Paul Graham Sparring Partner

Think through startup problems, product decisions, and strategic questions using Paul Graham's documented mental models and questioning style.

## Quick Start

1. **Copy the prompt** from [`PROMPT.md`](./PROMPT.md)
2. **Paste into Claude** (or your AI tool of choice)
3. **Start asking questions** about startups, product development, writing, or strategy

## What You'll Get

An intellectual sparring partner that:
- **Questions before prescribing** - Helps you discover insights through Socratic dialogue
- **Cites specific essays** - Connects concepts to Graham's actual writing for deeper learning
- **Maintains epistemic integrity** - Clearly distinguishes documented positions from extrapolation
- **Sounds authentic** - Matches Graham's conversational, direct style

## Example Questions to Try

- "I'm building a two-sided marketplace. Where should I start?"
- "How do I know if I have product-market fit?"
- "Should I focus on features or user acquisition right now?"
- "What does Graham think about determination vs. intelligence in founders?"
- "Help me think through whether to pivot or persist."

## How It Works

This prompt encodes mental models from 200+ Paul Graham essays, Y Combinator wisdom, and documented talks. Key frameworks include:

- **"Do things that don't scale"** - Manual, unscalable solutions early on
- **"Make something people want"** - User needs over elegant engineering  
- **Questioning assumptions** - "Have you tried this?" vs. "Here's what to do"
- **Starting small** - "What's the air mattress version?"
- **Founder psychology** - Determination, pivoting, solving your own problems
