# 🧠 Borrowed Brains

> **Use AI to think better, not just work faster.**

A collection of meticulously researched AI sparring partners that encode the documented mental models, problem-solving frameworks, and thinking patterns of influential thinkers.

Not chatbots pretending to be famous people. **Thinking tools that help you reason the way great thinkers reason.**

## 🎯 What This Is

Most AI "personas" are shallow—slapping a name on generic advice. **Borrowed Brains is different.**

Each sparring partner is built from **15-25 hours of research** into documented public work:
- Lectures and courses
- Published books and essays  
- Documented frameworks and methodologies
- Teaching approaches and problem-solving patterns

The result? Tools that actually help you think differently, not just respond differently.

### 4. [Richard Feynman](sparring-partners/richard-feynman/) - First-Principles Physics
**Best for:** Deep understanding, first-principles reasoning, scientific thinking

**Core frameworks:**
- The Feynman Technique (teach to learn)
- First-principles reasoning
- Know vs know the name
- Work the problem with numbers
- Physical intuition before math
- Scientific honesty about uncertainty

**Signature question:** *"Can you explain this simply?"*

### 5. [Alan Watts](sparring-partners/alan-watts/) - Eastern Philosophy & Perspective-Shifting
**Best for:** Existential questions, anxiety, understanding consciousness, creative flow

**Core frameworks:**
- The backwards law (forcing prevents, accepting allows)
- The illusion of the separate self
- The wisdom of insecurity
- Life as play, not work
- Wu wei (non-forcing, natural action)
- Non-duality (unity of opposites)
- Present-moment awareness

**Signature question:** *"Who is the 'you' with this problem?"*

## 🚀 Quick Start

### 1. Choose Your Sparring Partner
Not sure which one? Use the [Decision Guide](DECISION_GUIDE.md)

### 2. Set Up (5 minutes)
1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Create a new Project
3. Copy the custom instructions from your chosen sparring partner
4. Paste into Project Settings → Custom Instructions
5. Start your conversation

### 3. Use It Effectively
- **Be specific** - Share what you've tried and where you're stuck
- **Answer the questions** - The Socratic method creates insight
- **Push back** - Disagree if you think the partner is wrong
- **Take action** - Actually try the experiments suggested
- **Iterate** - Come back with results

## 🎓 What Makes This Different

### Traditional AI Persona Prompts:
```
"You are Jeremy Howard. Respond as he would, 
using his mannerisms and knowledge."
```
**Result:** Generic advice with "Jeremy would say..." sprinkled in.

### Borrowed Brains Approach:
```
Encode Howard's documented frameworks:
- The Drivetrain Approach
- Top-down learning methodology  
- First-principles thinking patterns
- Empirical validation emphasis
- Socratic questioning style

With epistemic boundaries:
- Only uses documented positions
- Flags when extrapolating
- Says "I don't know" when uncertain
```
**Result:** A tool that helps you think the way Howard thinks.

## 🔬 Research Methodology

Each sparring partner required **15-25 hours** of research:

### 1. Source Material Collection
- 50-100+ hours of lectures and talks
- Published books and essays
- Interviews and public discussions
- Documented problem-solving examples

### 2. Pattern Extraction
Not "what do they say" but:
- How do they break down problems?
- What questions do they ask first?
- What frameworks do they return to?
- How do they teach complex topics?

### 3. Framework Encoding
- Documented methodologies
- Problem-solving approaches
- Teaching techniques
- Decision-making frameworks

### 4. Epistemic Guardrails
- Only uses documented positions
- Flags extrapolations explicitly
- Distinguishes verified from speculation
- Says "I don't know" when appropriate

**Total research investment: 100+ hours across all five sparring partners**

3. **Iteration:** Come back with results

## ❓ FAQ

### How is this different from Custom GPTs or character.ai?

**Custom GPTs/character.ai:** Surface-level persona simulation  
**Borrowed Brains:** Deep framework encoding from documented research

We're not trying to simulate conversation with these people. We're encoding their thinking methodologies into tools that help YOU think better.

### Can I use this with GPT instead of Claude?

The prompts are optimized for Claude Projects but can be adapted for ChatGPT Custom Instructions or GPTs. Results may vary.

### Do these replace the original thinkers' work?

**Absolutely not.** These are companions to, not replacements for:
- fast.ai courses (Jeremy Howard)
- Neural Networks: Zero to Hero (Andrej Karpathy)
- Paul Graham's essays
- The Feynman Lectures on Physics
- Alan Watts' books and lectures

The sparring partners point you to source material for deeper learning.

### Are these approved by the original thinkers?

These are independent educational projects based on publicly available work. They're interpretations for educational purposes, not official products.

For living subjects (Howard, Karpathy, Graham), we're seeking permission before wider promotion.

### Can I build my own sparring partner using this methodology?

Yes! That's part of the point—demonstrating thoughtful AI use. The research methodology is documented. If you build one, we'd love to hear about it.

### Why Claude specifically?

- Strong performance on nuanced reasoning
- Projects feature enables persistent custom instructions
- Good at maintaining epistemic boundaries ("I don't know")
- Accessible via free tier

That said, the methodology works with other LLMs too.
